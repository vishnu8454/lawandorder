Consultations

You can schedule a consultation through our Contact page, by calling our office directly, or by sending us an email. We respond to all enquiries within one business day.

Once your enquiry is received, our team will confirm a suitable time and advise on any documents you should bring to make the consultation as productive as possible.

To make the most of your consultation, please bring:

  • Any relevant documents — contracts, notices, court orders, correspondence
  • A brief written summary of your situation (optional but helpful)
  • A list of questions you'd like answered
  • Government-issued photo ID

If your matter is complex, we may ask you to share documents in advance so our advocates can review them before your appointment.

Yes. We offer consultations via video call and telephone for clients who are unable to attend in person, whether due to location, health, or time constraints. Please mention your preference when booking and we will arrange accordingly.

Absolutely. Everything you share with us during a consultation is protected by attorney-client privilege and is held in strict confidence. We will never disclose your information to any third party without your express consent, except where required by law.

Fees & Billing

Our fee structure depends on the nature and complexity of the matter. We offer:

  • Fixed fees — for defined, straightforward matters
  • Hourly billing — for complex or open-ended matters
  • Retainer arrangements — for ongoing advisory clients

All fees will be clearly explained and agreed upon in writing before we commence work on your matter. There are no hidden charges.

We offer an initial consultation at a nominal fee that is applied toward the cost of your engagement if you choose to proceed. Please contact us directly to confirm current consultation rates, as these may vary by matter type.

Yes. After reviewing the facts of your matter, we will provide you with a written cost estimate or fee agreement before proceeding. While estimates for complex litigation may have ranges, we believe in full transparency about potential costs at the outset.

We accept bank transfers (NEFT/RTGS/IMPS), UPI payments, and cheques. Payment terms will be set out in your engagement letter. For ongoing retainer clients, we issue monthly invoices.

Legal Process

The duration of a legal matter depends on its nature and complexity. A simple contract review may be completed in days, while litigation before the High Court or Supreme Court can span months to years depending on court schedules, the number of parties, and procedural steps involved.

We will give you a realistic timeline estimate at the start and keep you informed at every stage of your matter.

Yes, wherever possible. You will be assigned a lead advocate who will be your primary point of contact and will have full knowledge of your matter. Where specialist input is needed, our team works collaboratively — but your lead advocate remains responsible for the overall management of your case.

We provide regular updates by phone, email, or in person — whichever you prefer. For court matters, you will receive a report after every hearing. You are also welcome to contact your advocate directly at any time with questions or concerns.

This depends on the nature of your matter. In many civil proceedings, your advocate can appear on your behalf without requiring your personal presence at every hearing. However, certain stages — such as recording of evidence, cross-examination, or settlement conferences — may require you to be present. We will advise you clearly about when your attendance is required.
